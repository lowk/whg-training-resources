Some population-genetic statistics

If you followed the basic simulation section you'll know that random inheritance patterns in the population cause haplotypes to be lost over time, and others to increase in frequency. As a result genetic diversity is lost. Let's now try to measure that diversity and see how it behaves.

Key popgen metrics​

Before starting let's define some notation. Let's use hih_ihi​ be mean the haplotype of individual iii, and hi(l)h_{i}(l)hi​(l) to mean the allele carried by haplotype hih_ihi​ at SNP lll. And I(⋅)I(\cdot)I(⋅) will denote the indicator function, which is 111 or 000 according to whether the condition is true.

The decay of heterozygosity​

A slightly amazing fact about population models like this is that - even though the behaviour of the model is highly stochastic, still regular behaviour emerges on average. That's true for the heterozygosity. To see this, consider two individuals randomly chosen at generation t+1t+1t+1. There's a 1/N1/N1/N chance they 'picked' the same parent at time ttt - if so they definitely carry the same haplotype. On the other hand, there's a (1−1N)\left(1-\frac{1}{N}\right)(1−N1​) chance they picked different parents - in which case the chance of having inhereting different haplotypes is the same as the chance the parents had different haplltpye - that is, HlH_lHl​. This leads to the recurrence relation:

Hl+1=(1−1N)HlH_{l+1} = \left( 1 - \frac{1}{N}\right) H_lHl+1​=(1−N1​)Hl​
